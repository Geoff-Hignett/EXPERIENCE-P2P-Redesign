What is peer-to-peer lending?

First appearing in the UK in 2005, peer-to-peer lending is an new way to earn interest. Financial institutions are removed from the equation, meaning you can lend to individuals and businesses for a certain amount of time at a particular rate. These rates are always higher (in some cases much higher) than what the high street banks have been able to offer in the last decade.

The first UK peer-to-peer lending company to offer this was Zopa. Launching in 2005, they had steady growth and came through the financial crisis without losing any investors funds. In 2017 they became the first UK peer-to-peer lender to exceed Â£2bn in loans.

As of 2020 there are over 65 peer-to-peer lending companies in the UK. The majority have yielded positive returns for all their investors. A few have been unsuccessful.
